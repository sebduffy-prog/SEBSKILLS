# Media strategy

Media strategy decides **where, when, how often, and against
whom** a brand shows up — and **why each choice exists**. It is
the bridge between the comms strategy (what we want to say) and
the media plan (the exact GRPs, impressions, and line items).
This skill produces the bridge: channel roles, the funnel logic,
the flighting principles, the success measures. It does not
produce buy specs.

## When to use

- The comms strategy is in flight and the question becomes "how"
- A client is asking for a channel POV before a media auction
- We're rebriefing an existing plan and need to defend the shape
- We're pitching and need to demonstrate channel thinking, not
  just creative thinking
- A new product launch needs an end-to-end orchestration logic

**Don't use this skill** for: programmatic line-item plans
(handled by the buyers), retail/trade marketing calendars (a
different discipline), or pure SEO/CRM tactics (subset disciplines).

## The four media questions

A media strategy is not done until each is one sentence.

1. **Who are we reaching, and how do we know we've reached them?**
2. **What does each channel exist to do?** (no role = cut it)
3. **What's the brand / activation balance, and the rhythm?**
4. **What's the proof we'll watch — and what would make us
   re-plan?**

## The principles that should be in every plan

These are the load-bearing ideas to lean on. Quote them by name
when defending a choice.

| Principle | Source | What it tells you |
|---|---|---|
| **Mental & physical availability** | Sharp / Ehrenberg-Bass | Penetration drives growth; reach broadly, refresh memory structures |
| **60/40 long-to-short** | Binet & Field, IPA | ~60% of paid spend on brand-building, ~40% on activation, varies by category (FMCG ~60/40, retail ~55/45, finance ~50/50, B2B ~46/54). Re-check the current IPA cut for the category |
| **ESOV (Excess Share of Voice)** | Jones / Binet-Field | A brand's SOV should exceed its SOM by ~10pts to grow share |
| **Attention adjusted reach** | Karen Nelson-Field / Amplified Intelligence / dentsu CCS | Impressions are a supply unit, not a demand unit — adjust by platform attention seconds |
| **Distinctive brand assets** | Romaniuk / Ehrenberg-Bass | Salience is built by *consistent* distinctive cues across channels |
| **Recency / continuity** | Ephron / IPA | For category-entry decisions (FMCG, QSR, fuel), continuous low-frequency presence beats short heavy bursts |
| **Diminishing returns curve** | every MMM ever | Past the saturation point, additional GRPs/impressions don't earn — find the elbow |
| **Cross-channel multiplier** | IPA Effectiveness Databank | More channels → larger effect, up to ~5–6 channels — beyond that returns flatten |
| **Share of search as leading indicator** | Les Binet | SoS tends to lead SoM by 6–12 months; useful KPI proxy |
| **Long → short asymmetry** | Field | Activation spend without brand erodes long-run elasticities; the activation-only plan is the worst plan |

## Workflow

### Step 1 — Define who you're trying to reach

In media terms, **who** has two layers:

- **Buyable audience** — the spec a buyer can target (age, sex,
  income, geography, behaviour, custom seg)
- **Strategic audience** — the human (from
  [[advertising-strategy]])

If the two don't reconcile, say so. A brief that says "women
25–54" but the strategy is about "first-time mums" will buy the
wrong reach.

### Step 2 — Quantify the prize

For each audience layer, get on one page:

- **Size** — universe, in millions
- **Reachability** — % reachable through standard channels at
  realistic SOV
- **Concentration** — which channels over-index for this audience
  (TGI / Touchpoints / GWI / first-party data)
- **Cost-per-reach** — rough order-of-magnitude CPMs by channel
  for this audience
- **Penetration of category** vs penetration of brand — defines
  the upside

Quantify before designing the mix. The numbers usually re-shape
the plan.

### Step 3 — Set the brand / activation balance

Default to a sector-appropriate split, then justify any deviation:

| Category | Brand : Activation default |
|---|---|
| FMCG | 60 : 40 |
| Retail | 55 : 45 |
| Finance | 50 : 50 |
| Auto | 65 : 35 |
| Subscription / DTC | 50 : 50 (varies by maturity) |
| B2B | 46 : 54 |
| Charity | 60 : 40 |
| Telco | 55 : 45 |

Reasons to deviate:

- New product launch → tilt brand for the first 6–12 weeks
- Distress / promotional period → tilt activation, time-limited
- Pre-Christmas / end-of-quarter → activation tilt
- Post-Christmas / sale comedown → brand re-build tilt

Deviations are **time-bound**. "Activation-heavy forever" is the
plan that erodes the elasticity Field warned about.

### Step 4 — Assign a role to every channel

No role = cut the channel. Use a single sentence per channel:

```
[CHANNEL] exists in this plan to [verb] [audience], when they
are [state of mind / context], so that [comms job].
```

Examples:

- **TV** — exists to put the brand in front of 65% of category
  buyers at 1+ in week one, when they are co-viewing and
  receptive to story, so that the launch idea takes hold
  emotionally.
- **YouTube CTV** — exists to extend TV reach into the
  ~25% of category buyers who are light TV viewers, when they
  lean-back on connected TV, so that frequency is balanced
  across age cohorts.
- **OOH (D6 + roadside)** — exists to dramatise the brand's
  scale and the campaign's distinctive asset, in public space,
  so that the campaign feels famous before it is famous.
- **Search (brand + generic)** — exists to capture demand the
  brand work has stimulated, at the moment of category entry, so
  no money leaks to competitors.
- **Paid social (Meta, TikTok)** — exists to drive frequency
  on the creative-led story among 18–34s who under-index on TV,
  so reach holds across the cohort.
- **Retail media** — exists to convert interest at the digital
  shelf, so the brand earns the trolley click.
- **Owned (CRM / app / .com)** — exists to deepen relationship
  with the 11% who are buyers already.
- **Earned (PR / influence)** — exists to give the campaign
  cultural permission to be talked about by people who aren't
  paid to talk about it.

### Step 5 — Funnel logic

Map channels to the funnel **honestly**. Most channels do more
than one job; pick the primary.

```
AWARENESS / SALIENCE    TV, BVOD, CTV, OOH (large format), audio,
                        cinema, paid social reach buys, sponsorships
CONSIDERATION / MEANING TV (sequencing), online video (long-form),
                        print/native (long-form), influencer,
                        PR features, paid social engagement
ACTIVATION / DEMAND     Search (generic), paid social (DR),
                        affiliate, retail media, addressable TV,
                        out-of-home near-store
CONVERSION / LOYALTY    CRM, app, search (brand), .com,
                        loyalty programme, customer service
```

The full-funnel claim is fine, but only if each channel has a
specific funnel role written down. "Full-funnel" with no role
mapping = no plan.

### Step 6 — Flight the plan

Pick the rhythm and justify it:

- **Always-on (continuous)** — recency-driven categories (FMCG,
  QSR, fuel). Low weekly weight, ~52 weeks
- **Pulsed / heavy-up** — seasonal categories (Christmas retail,
  summer travel) — keep a base + pulse for moments
- **Burst** — launch, news moments. Only when the brand has
  stored salience to capitalise on
- **Sequential / sequencing** — sometimes-on, lighter spend
  weeks paired with creative sequencing for memory effects

Whatever the shape, **named moments** drive the calendar. List
the 6–12 moments the year hangs on (launches, seasonal pulses,
sponsorships, sport, retail moments, news beats). Default to too
few — over-moment-ing dilutes weight.

### Step 7 — Measurement framework

A media strategy without measurement is a wish. Three layers:

| Layer | Examples |
|---|---|
| **Output** | Reach (1+, 3+), frequency, GRPs, impressions, video completion, attention seconds |
| **Outcome** | Spontaneous awareness, prompted recall, brand-linked recall, consideration shift, share of search |
| **Business** | New customers, sales lift, share of category, ROI / iROAS, LTV |

For each, declare:

- Baseline
- Target
- Source
- Frequency of reporting
- The "we'd re-plan if…" threshold

### Step 8 — Test-and-learn design

Every plan needs at least one test slot. Decide:

- **Question** — what specifically are we trying to learn?
- **Design** — A/B, holdout, geo-split, matched-market,
  conversion-lift, brand-lift, incrementality, MMM
- **Power** — minimum spend / weeks needed for a readable result
- **Decision rule** — what we'll do with the answer

A "test" with no decision rule is just spend.

## Output template

```
MEDIA STRATEGY — [client / project]
Version, date

1. The audience (buyable + strategic)
2. The prize (size, reachability, cost-to-reach)
3. The principles we're following (with WHY this brief)
4. The brand / activation split and the rhythm
5. The role of each channel (one sentence each)
6. The funnel logic
7. Flighting and named moments
8. Measurement framework (output / outcome / business)
9. The test-and-learn slot
10. Re-plan triggers
```

For pitch decks, compress to 3–5 slides:

```
1. Where the audience really is  →  reachability + concentration chart
2. The principles we're choosing  →  named, justified
3. The role of each channel       →  funnel map, one-line roles
4. The rhythm                     →  fly-chart + moments
5. How we'll prove it             →  KPI hierarchy + test slot
```

## Deliverable

This skill must end with a **written file**, not chat narration.
Ship the strategy as a document — default
`~/Desktop/[client]-media-strategy.docx` (use the `docx` skill),
or `.md` if the user prefers — carrying all ten output-template
sections. For a pitch, produce the compressed 3–5 slide `.pptx`
instead (use the `pptx` skill).

If inputs are missing (audience data, budget, category context),
still ship the scaffold: every section present, with the gaps
flagged `**[awaiting data: …]**` rather than left blank or
guessed. Never fabricate reachability, CPMs, or splits.

**Verify before done:** the file exists at the stated path, opens
cleanly, and a spot-check confirms each channel carries a written
role, the split is justified, and the measurement layer has
baselines/targets (or explicit `awaiting data` markers).

## Common mistakes to avoid

1. **Channel mix as solution.** "We'll do TV, social, OOH" is
   not a strategy — it's a list. Until each channel has a role,
   you don't have one.
2. **Buying impressions instead of attention.** Two 30-second
   spots with 5 sec attention each ≠ one 30-second spot with 10
   sec attention. Apply attention adjustments.
3. **Activation-heavy plans because they're easy to measure.**
   The thing you can attribute most easily is rarely the thing
   that grows the brand.
4. **"Always-on" as a brand-build default.** Recency benefits
   recency categories. For considered purchases, always-on can
   be inefficient.
5. **Treating SOV as the only voice metric.** Share of search,
   attention-adjusted reach, and quality of placement matter as
   much.
6. **Audience definition by media availability.** Don't pick
   "ABC1 25–54" because it's a media seg; pick it only if it
   maps to the strategic audience.
7. **Ignoring frequency caps and wear-out.** Frequency above
   ~6–8 per week on the same creative is usually past the elbow.
8. **One-channel-one-job rigidity.** A channel can do two things
   in two creatives; just say so.
9. **No re-plan triggers.** Plans should change when the data
   says they should. Pre-commit to that.
10. **Treating MMM as backward-looking only.** MMM outputs feed
    the next plan. Brief the MMM team *with* the next year's
    questions in mind, not just last year's.

## Useful starting numbers (sense-check defaults)

These are sense-checks, not prescriptions — pull current
benchmarks before quoting in a client deck.

- **Average attention seconds (Amplified Intelligence approx):**
  TV ~ 22s, BVOD ~ 13s, YouTube skippable ~ 5–9s, FB feed ~ 1–2s,
  Instagram feed ~ 1–2s, TikTok feed ~ 4–7s, OOH classic ~ 0.8s,
  D6 roadside ~ 1.5s
- **Attention-based effective frequency rules of thumb:** brand
  recall threshold around 2.5 attention-seconds per impression
- **CPM order of magnitude (UK 2025/26 ranges, adult universe):**
  TV ~ £4–15 per 30s; BVOD ~ £18–35; YouTube ~ £8–20; Meta ~
  £5–12; TikTok ~ £4–10; D6 OOH ~ £6–14
- **Search query share split:** brand vs generic mix tells you
  where the funnel is starving — if generics are >70% and brand
  searches are flat, brand work isn't doing enough

Treat as priors. Pull current numbers before quoting.
